Rebuilding a $12k/month HVAC Google Ads account that wasn't tracking a single conversion

The situation

This HVAC company operates in the Pacific Northwest, serving both residential and light commercial customers. They had been spending $10,000 to $14,000 per month on Google Ads for two years, managed by a generalist marketing agency. The agency was sending monthly reports showing click volume and impressions. There was no data on leads or revenue from ads.

The owner could not say with confidence how much revenue the ads were responsible for or what the cost per booked job was. He came to us when a competitor opened a second location in his primary service area and he wanted to know if he was in a defensible position.

What the audit found

There was no conversion tracking of any kind in the Google Ads account. No call tracking, no form submission goals, no imported conversions. The campaigns were running on manual CPC with no optimization signal. Every bid adjustment the previous agency had made was based on cost and click volume, not on what those clicks were producing.

The campaign structure had 23 active ad groups across four campaigns, several of which had overlapping keywords competing with each other. Quality Scores on the primary service keywords were between 3 and 5 out of 10, driving up cost per click significantly. The landing page for all campaigns was the homepage, which had a pop-up offering a newsletter signup appearing within three seconds of page load. The site loaded in 6.2 seconds on mobile.

The rebuild

We started with tracking because nothing else was worth doing until we could see what was happening. We set up CallRail with dynamic number insertion on the website and landing pages, connected to Google Ads as a conversion import. We configured Google Tag Manager to fire a form conversion event on successful submissions, with a 90-second minimum call duration threshold for call conversions.

With tracking in place, we ran the existing campaigns for two weeks before touching the structure. We found that approximately 35% of spend was going to searches that had no reasonable path to a service booking. We consolidated the 23 ad groups into seven, organized by service type: AC repair, AC installation, furnace repair, furnace installation, heat pump, maintenance, and emergency. Each had dedicated landing pages built from scratch, loading under two seconds on mobile. The homepage pop-up was removed for all paid traffic using a URL parameter exclusion.

Results at 90 days

In the first 30 days, just having conversion tracking in place changed the owner relationship with the account. For the first time, he could see that the furnace repair campaign was generating more leads per dollar than any other campaign, and that the maintenance campaign was generating leads at a cost that was not justified by the average job value. Budget was reallocated. The maintenance campaign was paused. The AC installation campaign was scaled up going into summer.

By day 90, the account was generating 4.7 times the tracked revenue per dollar spent compared to the estimated pre-audit baseline. Monthly spend was $12,400, essentially unchanged. The improvement was structural, not budgetary.

I spent two years not knowing if this was working. Now I know down to the campaign which jobs came from ads. That is a completely different way of running the business.

The competitor situation

Within three months of the rebuild, the client had enough data to see that his cost per booked job on Google Ads was meaningfully lower than what his competitor would need to spend to displace him on the same keywords, based on Quality Score differentials. A well-structured account with high Quality Scores is structurally cheaper to operate than a poorly structured one competing for the same traffic.
