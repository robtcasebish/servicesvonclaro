Landscaping: Service area mismatch, seasonal budget gaps, no tracking

Primary leak

Spring demand flowing into poorly targeted service areas. Budget running in off-season with no shutoff logic. Zero tracking on which campaigns generated actual quote requests.

What the Audit Found

Spend reaching the wrong areas at the wrong time

A landscaping company running year-round campaigns without seasonal adjustment logic. Spring and fall were peak demand periods: but budget was allocated evenly across all months. Off-season clicks were generating clicks from homeowners not ready to buy for another two months.

Geographic targeting was set to a radius that extended well beyond the company's actual service area. Clicks coming from 40+ minutes away: jobs that would never be booked because of travel time: were consuming a significant portion of monthly budget.

  • Service area targeting set to a 40-mile radius: actual service area was 15 miles
  • Budget consistent year-round: no peak-season scaling, no off-season reduction
  • No conversion tracking: quote request forms not attributed to any campaign
  • Single landing page for all services: lawn care, landscape design, seasonal cleanups: no service-specific pages
  • Quote requests going to email, manually followed up with no automation

What Was Rebuilt

Service area tightened, seasonal budget logic added, tracking installed

  • Geographic targeting tightened to actual service area: postal codes and city-level targeting replacing the radius approach
  • Seasonal budget calendar created: peak budget in spring and fall, reduced in summer, minimal in winter with brand-only campaigns
  • Service-specific landing pages created: lawn care maintenance, landscape design quotes, seasonal cleanups: separate pages, separate conversion paths
  • Form conversion tracking installed: every quote request attributed to the campaign that drove it
  • Automated SMS confirmation sent to every form submission within 2 minutes
  • Negative keywords added: DIY lawn care, grass seed, fertilizer, tools, rental equipment
Check Your Account

What to check in a landscaping account

  • Does your geographic targeting match your actual service area, or is it broader than you can realistically serve?
  • Does your budget scale up during peak demand months and reduce in the off-season?
  • Are lawn maintenance, landscape design, and seasonal cleanup in separate campaigns?
  • Can you see which campaigns generated quote requests last month?
  • Do quote requests get an automated response within minutes, or sit in an inbox until someone checks it?
