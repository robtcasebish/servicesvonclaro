How a mid-size roofing company cut cost per booked estimate by 54%

Fragmented campaigns, no call tracking, and a homepage as the only landing page. A full system rebuild over 90 days changed the economics of their paid acquisition.

The situation

This client is a roofing company operating across two Ontario markets with 12 crews and a mix of residential replacement and commercial flat roofing work. They had been running Google Ads independently for three years, spending approximately $6,000 per month, and had a general sense that it was generating some leads but no clear data on how many or at what cost.

When we audited the account, we found seven active campaigns, six of which were running on broad match keywords with no negative keyword lists. All seven campaigns sent traffic to the company homepage.

What the audit found

Conversion tracking was set up but misconfigured. A Google Analytics goal was firing on every page load of the contact page, not just on successful form submissions. The account was recording hundreds of conversions per month that were actually just pageviews. The real conversion rate, once we fixed the tracking, was closer to 2.4%.

There was no call tracking at all. The phone number in the ads and on the website was the company main line with no forwarding number attached. Phone calls from ads were completely invisible. The search term report showed significant spend on irrelevant queries including "roofing jobs near me," "roofing materials wholesale," "how to fix a roof yourself," and "roofing school Ontario."

The rebuild

We paused all existing campaigns and started from scratch with a new account structure organized around intent and service type: emergency repair, replacement and installation, and service area campaigns geo-targeted to each market. Every campaign used exact and phrase match only. We built a starter negative keyword list of 140 terms and committed to reviewing the search term report weekly.

We built dedicated landing pages for each campaign type. The emergency repair page led with urgency and had the phone number prominently displayed. The replacement page led with the estimate process and addressed the most common objections in the copy. We set up CallRail for call tracking with dynamic number insertion on both landing pages. A basic lead follow-up sequence sent an automated SMS to every form submission within two minutes of receipt.

Results at 90 days

Within the first 30 days, wasted spend dropped significantly as the negative keyword list took effect. Cost per click actually increased slightly because we were now competing only on high-intent keywords. But conversion rate went from 2.4% to 7.8% because the landing pages were purpose-built for the searches they were receiving.

By day 90, the account was generating 3.1 times the number of booked estimates per month compared to the baseline period, at 54% of the previous cost per booked estimate. Monthly ad spend was essentially the same. The improvement came entirely from the system, not from spending more.

We were spending the same amount. The phone just started ringing more. And we could actually tell which campaigns were doing it.

What made the difference

The call tracking setup was the single most impactful change. Once phone calls were visible, we could see that the emergency repair campaign was generating three times more calls per dollar than the replacement campaign. That insight directly informed budget allocation in a way that had not been possible before. The landing pages mattered more than any keyword change. The homepage had a 2.4% conversion rate. The purpose-built landing pages converted at 7 to 9% depending on the campaign.
