Where the Leak Starts

Most lead gen problems happen after the click.

More ad spend will not fix a broken lead system. If calls are missed, landing pages are generic, and follow-up is slow, your campaigns are doing their job the system behind them is not.

The companies spending the most are not always the ones booking the most jobs. The ones winning have a system that converts intent into booked work at every stage.

Common finding: 35 to 50 percent of ad spend in a typical home services account is consumed by irrelevant searches, poor landing page match, or leads that go cold before any follow-up happens.

The Six Failure Points

Where the system breaks and what we rebuild.

We do not run isolated campaigns. We diagnose the full system from click to booked job, fix each failure point, and operate it continuously so performance compounds over time.

01

Campaign Structure

Ad spend

Rebuilt around high-intent keywords for each service and service area. Exact and phrase match only. Negative keyword lists reviewed weekly so budget reaches buyers not researchers, job seekers, or wholesale queries.

02

Landing Pages

Conversion

Dedicated pages for each campaign: fast on mobile, matched to the ad promise, one clear path to a call or estimate request. No more paying for clicks that land on a homepage built for someone who already knows you.

03

Call and Form Tracking

Tracking

Dynamic call tracking numbers on every page. Every missed call logged. Form conversion events that fire correctly. Full attribution from ad click to phone call to submitted estimate request so nothing is invisible.

04

Lead Follow-Up and Routing

Speed to lead

Automated SMS within 2 minutes of every inbound lead. Routing rules that get the right inquiry to the right person immediately. No estimate request goes cold while your crew is on a job site.

05

Attribution to Booked Jobs

Attribution

Connect ad spend to actual booked work. Every job in your CRM tagged to its ad source. Know which campaigns, keywords, and service areas are generating revenue not just clicks that never became estimates.

06

Reporting and Optimization

Operations

Weekly account reviews. Monthly cost-per-booked-job reporting by campaign and service area. Continuous bid, creative, and budget optimization based on what is actually generating booked work not impressions.

What Changes When the Leaks Are Fixed

Consistent patterns across home services accounts.

These are patterns we see repeatedly after fixing the six failure points. Results vary by market, budget, and starting point but the direction is consistent.

Ad Spend

Wasted spend drops when broad match is replaced with intent-based keywords

  • Irrelevant queries blocked by a maintained negative keyword list
  • Cost per click decreases as Quality Scores improve with better landing page match
  • Budget concentrates on searches from homeowners ready to book, not browse

Landing Pages

Conversion rate increases when traffic lands on purpose-built pages

  • Service-specific pages outperform homepages consistently across trades
  • Mobile-optimized pages with a single CTA reduce abandonment
  • Cost per estimate falls as the same spend converts more visitors into requests

Call Tracking

Visibility into which campaigns are driving booked jobs, not just clicks

  • Call tracking reveals which keywords and campaigns generate real inbound volume
  • Smart Bidding improves materially when fed accurate conversion signal
  • Budget reallocation becomes data-driven instead of based on gut feel

Follow-Up

More estimates booked when lead response drops from 37 minutes to 2

  • Automated SMS within 2 minutes of every inbound lead, regardless of crew schedule
  • Fewer leads go cold while the owner is on a job site or driving between stops
  • Higher booking rate from the same lead volume without increasing ad spend
