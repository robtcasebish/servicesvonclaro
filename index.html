The Problem

Most lead gen problems happen after the click.

More ad spend will not fix a broken lead system. If calls are missed, landing pages are generic, and follow-up is slow, your campaigns are doing their job — the system behind them is not.

The companies spending the most are not always the ones booking the most jobs. The ones winning have a system that converts intent into booked work at every stage.

Common finding: 35 to 50 percent of ad spend in a typical home services account is consumed by irrelevant searches, poor landing page match, or leads that go cold before any follow-up happens.

What most home services accounts look like on arrival

  • Broad match keywords sending budget to DIY searchers and job applicants
  • All paid traffic landing on a homepage built for navigation, not conversion
  • Phone calls from ads not tracked — no attribution to campaigns or keywords
  • Leads sitting in an inbox for hours while crews are on job sites

Fixed system

What the same account looks like when the system works

  • Intent-based keywords matched to each service and service area
  • Dedicated landing pages for each campaign with a single path to a call or estimate
  • Call tracking on every touchpoint — calls attributed back to campaign and keyword
  • Automated SMS within minutes of every inbound lead, before it goes cold
  • Monthly reporting on cost per booked job by campaign and service type
  • Budget decisions driven by which campaigns generate actual revenue
